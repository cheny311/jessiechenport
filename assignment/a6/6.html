This is a data portrait that visualizes personal data through an animated character.

The character features:

  • Red hair with individual strands
  • Animated mouth that displays data as a wave form
  • Interactive arms that follow your mouse movement
  • Data values: [90, 67, 90, 55, 82, 100, 80]

For this data portrait, I used my mood as data and turned it into the shape of the mouth on my character. The mouth moves like a heartbeat, showing how emotions can rise and fall in small waves. I built this piece based on one of my previous characters, using her as a base for this visual expression. While making it, I realized it helps me to keep an eye on my own mood — not to control it, but just to notice it. Through tracking my mood, I learned that feelings shift quietly all the time — sometimes without me even realizing it. Turning those emotions into data made me more aware of them, but also made them feel a little less personal, almost mechanical. Something was gained — I could see myself more clearly, from a small distance. But something was also lost — the warmth and texture of how emotions actually feel in the moment. Still, I like how the mouth keeps moving, as if it’s breathing. It reminds me that data can’t capture everything, but it can still hold traces of how I was feeling — and that’s enough.
